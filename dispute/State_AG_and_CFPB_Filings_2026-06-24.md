## A. California Attorney General Consumer Protection Section

**Filing destination**: California Attorney General Public Inquiry
Unit, `oag.ca.gov/contact/consumer-complaint-against-business`

**Jurisdiction basis**: Creality (Shenzhen Creality 3D Technology
Co., Ltd.) maintains a U.S. presence at City of Industry, California.
The U.S. corporate presence is the operational locus for consumer
disputes, customer service, and U.S. retail distribution. Conduct
toward California consumers and conduct from the California-based
corporate presence is within California consumer-protection
enforcement scope.

**Complaint summary**: per the public case file at
`github.com/jpuckett11/CrealityK2Plus_sweep` and the parallel FTC
complaint, Creality engages in:

- Deceptive consumer-IoT product practices (consent UI theater,
  marketing-to-reality gap on data collection scope)
- Account-identity-tagged adversarial restriction targeting
  individual investigative consumers
- Chargeback-defeat conduct routing disputes through an unadmitted
  insurer intermediary (Seel, Inc.) subject to active class action
  in N.D. Cal.
- Refund payment-rail bypass requests defeating consumer chargeback
  protection

**Specific California consumer-protection statutes implicated**:

- California Consumer Privacy Act / California Privacy Rights Act
  (CCPA/CPRA): per-device identifier-bound image and behavioral
  data collection without specific consumer notice or specific
  consent
- California Unfair Competition Law (Business and Professions Code
  §17200): deceptive marketing-to-reality gap, account-identity
  retaliation
- California False Advertising Law (Business and Professions Code
  §17500): marketing claims about consumer protections not matching
  operational reality

**Requested action**: investigation, public consumer advisory, and
appropriate enforcement against the California-based corporate
presence of Shenzhen Creality 3D Technology Co., Ltd.

## B. Tennessee Attorney General Consumer Protection Division

**Filing destination**: Tennessee Attorney General Consumer Affairs
Division, `tn.gov/consumer/complaints.html`

**Jurisdiction basis**: the submitter is a Tennessee resident. The
underlying transaction occurred between a Tennessee consumer and
Creality. Conduct affecting Tennessee residents through inter-state
e-commerce is within Tennessee consumer-protection enforcement
scope.

**Complaint summary**: same underlying conduct as the California
filing above, framed for Tennessee consumer-protection enforcement
authority.

**Specific Tennessee consumer-protection statutes implicated**:

- Tennessee Consumer Protection Act (T.C.A. §47-18-104): unfair or
  deceptive acts or practices affecting Tennessee consumers,
  including specifically the conduct categories enumerated in
  §47-18-104(b) covering misrepresentation, false advertising, and
  refusal of refund where the goods were not provided

**Specific conduct**:

- Sold and accepted payment for a Creality CR-Scan Otter 3D scanner
  that was never delivered to the Tennessee consumer
- Sold the scanner via SpeedX (non-major carrier) while the rest
  of the same order shipped via FedEx, structurally producing
  weaker proof-of-delivery for the high-value item
- Provided shifting account of delivery ("front door" then
  "driveway" only after the front-door claim was challenged by the
  consumer's gated-property facts)
- Routed the chargeback through Seel, Inc. (currently under federal
  class action in N.D. Cal. case 4:26-cv-01336 for operating as an
  unadmitted insurer in California)
- Requested refund via direct banking transfer outside the original
  card-network payment channel, defeating both card-network
  consumer protections and BNPL-lender (Affirm) loan recovery

**Requested action**: Tennessee Consumer Affairs Division
investigation, complaint mediation, and appropriate enforcement.

## C. Consumer Financial Protection Bureau

**Filing destination**: CFPB consumer complaint portal,
`consumerfinance.gov/complaint/`

**Filing category**: Money transfer, virtual currency, or money
service / Other money service problem / Money was not available
when promised / Issue with money I was supposed to receive

**Complaint summary**: documented merchant-side conduct that
prejudices both consumer chargeback protections AND BNPL-lender
loan recovery:

- Creality routed an active bank chargeback dispute through Seel,
  Inc. (subject of class action *Mickey v. Seel, Inc.*, N.D. Cal.
  case 4:26-cv-01336, alleging operation as an unadmitted insurer
  in California through a captive insurance subsidiary that does
  not actually insure consumers)
- Creality requested refund deposit via direct ACH, wire, or SWIFT
  transfer outside the original Affirm-funded card-network payment
  rail
- The off-rail refund pathway would prejudice Affirm's loan recovery
  (refund deposited to consumer's bank account does not return to
  Affirm; Affirm absorbs the loss while the consumer might or might
  not actually receive funds)
- The bidirectional fraud-vector finding in the OWG case file
  documents that the same operational pattern Creality is
  exhibiting is exploitable in both directions: merchant-side
  chargeback defeat AND consumer-side BNPL-default fraud through
  the same architectural mechanism

**Requested action**: CFPB review of the conduct affecting BNPL
lender (Affirm) and the consumer protection in BNPL-financed
purchases. The conduct is potentially relevant to the broader CFPB
attention on BNPL consumer-protection enforcement.

## D. California Department of Insurance complaint regarding Seel

**Filing destination**: California Department of Insurance complaint
portal, `insurance.ca.gov/01-consumers/101-help/`

**Complaint summary**: Creality's redirection of an active bank
chargeback dispute through Seel, Inc., presented as an "insurance
program," constitutes operational evidence of the structural
pattern alleged in the active class action *Mickey v. Seel, Inc.*,
N.D. Cal. case 4:26-cv-01336.

The case alleges Seel operates as an unadmitted insurer in
California in violation of California Insurance Code, through a
wholly-owned captive insurance subsidiary (Seel Insurance, Inc.)
that does not actually insure consumers but rather insures its
parent against contractual exposure.

This complaint is filed as a supplemental consumer-experience
documentation alongside (not duplicative of) the class action. The
submitter's specific experience documents:

- Creality's communication to the submitter on 2026-06-22:
  "Hello, we have contacted Seel Insurance to extend your case.
  Please submit your claim as soon as possible." (verbatim
  Creality language to a Tennessee consumer)
- The submitter's experience attempting to engage with Seel
  documentation requirements (police-report demand that local law
  enforcement declined to produce; biometric face-scan demand;
  documented public-record concerns about Seel's claim-handling
  practices including the Sitejabber-documented fabricated Venmo
  transaction ID case)

**Requested action**: California DOI review of merchant-routed
insurance-product distribution practices, particularly the
operational pattern of merchants routing active card-network
disputes through unadmitted-insurer intermediaries.
